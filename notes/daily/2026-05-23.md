## Core Takeaways

- [ ] **Blockchain is a state machine, not a database.** State advances block by block. Consensus lets trustless nodes agree on transitions. No central server controls it — this is the most fundamental difference from Web2.
- [ ] **Private key = control. Signature = authorization proof.** Web3 has no "reset password." Lose the key = lose control. A signature is not a "login confirmation" — it's a specific authorization for a specific action.
- [ ] **The most dangerous moment**: when a signature popup appears and you don't understand what you're signing. Products must show: purpose, chain ID, contract address, amount — this principle is critical for your future hackathon projects.
